Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for the Data Scientist role at the CDC Foundation's Workforce Acceleration Initiative because the chance to use data science to directly improve public health outcomes is exactly the kind of work I want to be doing. My brother is a physician and my sister is a psychologist, so I grew up around conversations about patient outcomes, population health, and the real consequences of bad or missing information. That upbringing shaped how seriously I take data quality and how much I care about getting the analysis right, not just technically, but in ways that actually help people make better decisions.

At Tekkii, I spent three years building and maintaining data systems that had to be accurate, reliable, and useful to non-technical stakeholders. I designed and managed databases across PostgreSQL, SQL Server, and MySQL, wrote complex queries and stored procedures, and built automated reporting pipelines that turned raw data into actionable insights for decision makers. For Kansas City Drayage, I built an EDI processing system from scratch that automated logistics data exchange and eliminated manual workflows entirely. I also led the backend and database transformation of KidsCloset.biz, a project that ultimately resulted in the platform's successful acquisition, and built the KC Sports Directory database, a community-facing project supported by the Kansas City Royals Foundation. Throughout all of this, I got very good at translating complex technical work into plain language for clients and stakeholders, which I know matters just as much as the analysis itself.

Before Tekkii, I worked as a Software Engineer at Allied Engineering Group, where I designed SQL Server schemas and wrote optimized queries supporting data analysis for more than 400 financial institutions across more than 40 countries. I maintained data integrity across high-volume transaction databases, built custom reports for clients with varying technical backgrounds, and learned how to work carefully within strict data governance and accuracy requirements. During my Master of Science in Computer Science at the University of Missouri-Kansas City, I built a pneumonia detection application using Python and deep learning on medical imaging data. That project brought together everything I care about, machine learning, real-world health data, and the idea that a well-built model can have a meaningful impact on someone's life.

What draws me to this specific role is the opportunity to apply statistical and machine learning methods to public health surveillance and population health measurement, work that has direct consequences for communities across Illinois. I am genuinely excited about collaborating with epidemiologists and public health professionals to make sure the analysis reflects the real-world context, not just the numbers on a screen. Contributing to a mission focused on strengthening the nation's public health infrastructure feels like exactly the right place to put this work to use.

Thank you for considering my application. I would love to discuss how my data science, machine learning, and data systems experience can contribute to the CDC Foundation's mission of building stronger, more data-driven public health agencies.

Sincerely,
Mohamad Abboud
