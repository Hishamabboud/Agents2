I am applying for the Senior Business Analyst role at New York University because I believe in what your Decision Support Group is doing: helping people across a large, complex institution make better decisions through reliable data. Throughout all of it, I worked closely with stakeholders to translate what they needed into something that actually worked.

Before that, at Allied Engineering Group in Beirut, I designed SQL Server schemas and built reports supporting data analysis for more than 400 financial institutions across more than 40 countries. I maintained data accuracy across high-volume SWIFT transaction databases, identified and resolved inconsistencies, and created custom reports and visualizations tailored to each client's specific needs. That environment taught me how to work carefully under pressure and communicate clearly with people who have no interest in technical details but need to trust their data completely. My master's degree in Computer Science from the University of Missouri-Kansas City deepened my technical foundation, with coursework in advanced database systems, and a capstone project where I built a pneumonia detection application using Python and medical imaging data.

NYU's scale and mission make this role genuinely exciting to me. Supporting a university means the data work you do touches students, faculty, researchers, and administrators all at once, and the stakes are real. I am drawn to the idea of being part of a team that helps the institution understand itself better, resolving discrepancies before they affect decisions, building tools that make the whole team more efficient, and being the kind of resource that people actually trust. That is exactly the kind of work I want to be doing.

Thank you for considering my application. I would love to discuss how my SQL, ETL, and business intelligence experience, along with my track record of user-centered documentation and training, can contribute to NYU's mission of enabling smarter, data-driven decisions across the university.
