Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for the Clinical Informatics Engineer role at Hackensack Meridian Health because the work of building data infrastructure that directly supports emergency medicine care is exactly the kind of problem I want to be solving. My brother is a physician and my sister is a psychologist, so I grew up around healthcare conversations at the dinner table. Those conversations gave me a real sense of what is at stake when data is unclear or a dashboard tells the wrong story. That background shapes how I think about every system I build.

At Tekkii, I spent three years doing work that maps closely to what this role requires. I designed and maintained SQL databases across PostgreSQL, SQL Server, and MySQL, built complex queries and stored procedures, and created automated reporting solutions that helped business stakeholders make better decisions with their data. I built interactive dashboards and self-service reporting tools so that non-technical users could explore their own data without needing to come back to engineering every time. For Kansas City Drayage, I built an EDI capture and processing system from scratch that eliminated manual workflows entirely. I also led the backend and database transformation of KidsCloset.biz, which resulted in the platform being acquired, and built the KC Sports Directory database, a community-facing tool supported by local health and sports organizations.

Before Tekkii, I worked as a Software Engineer at Allied Engineering Group, where I designed SQL Server schemas, wrote optimized queries, and generated custom reports for more than 400 financial institutions across more than 40 countries. That environment taught me how to keep data accurate and reliable at scale, how to communicate findings clearly to non-technical clients, and how to document processes thoroughly so nothing falls through the cracks. I earned my Master of Science in Computer Science from the University of Missouri-Kansas City, where I also built a pneumonia detection application using Python and medical imaging data. That project showed me firsthand how much the right analytical tool can matter in a clinical context, and it deepened my interest in applying data science to healthcare problems.

The Emergency Medicine setting here is particularly exciting to me because it is one of the highest-stakes environments in any hospital, and the opportunity to build simulation models, predictive tools, and quality improvement dashboards that help clinicians and leadership make faster and better decisions feels genuinely meaningful. I am drawn to Hackensack Meridian Health because of the seriousness with which the organization approaches innovation in care delivery, and I want to contribute to that. This is the kind of work that connects the technical skills I have been building for years to outcomes that actually matter.

Thank you for considering my application. I would love to discuss how my experience in data engineering, SQL infrastructure, and analytical reporting can contribute to Hackensack Meridian Health's mission of transforming emergency medicine care.
