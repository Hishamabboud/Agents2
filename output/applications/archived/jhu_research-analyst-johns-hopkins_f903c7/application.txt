Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for the Research Analyst role at Johns Hopkins University because JHU has long stood for rigorous, meaningful research that actually moves the needle on real-world problems, and I want to be part of that work. I believe that good research depends just as much on clean, well-structured data as it does on sharp ideas, and that is exactly where I can contribute. The opportunity to support research at an institution with Hopkins' reputation for impact is genuinely exciting to me.

At Tekkii, I spent three years doing the kind of data work this role calls for: designing and maintaining SQL databases across PostgreSQL, SQL Server, and MySQL; writing complex queries and stored procedures; and building automated reporting solutions that gave stakeholders clear, actionable information. I built an EDI processing system from scratch for Kansas City Drayage, which replaced a fully manual logistics data workflow with a reliable, automated pipeline. I also transformed the backend and database layer of KidsCloset.biz, a project that ended in the platform's successful acquisition, and built the KC Sports Directory database in collaboration with community partners including the Kansas City Royals Foundation. Across all of these projects, I worked closely with non-technical stakeholders to understand what they needed, then translated those needs into clean data solutions and clear documentation they could actually use.

Before Tekkii, I worked as a Software Engineer at Allied Engineering Group in Beirut, where I designed SQL Server schemas, optimized queries, and produced custom reports for more than 400 financial institutions across 40 countries. I maintained high-volume SWIFT transaction databases and took data quality seriously, running regular audits, catching inconsistencies, and making sure the information clients relied on was trustworthy. I later earned my Master of Science in Computer Science from the University of Missouri-Kansas City, where I built a pneumonia detection application using Python and medical imaging data, which deepened my appreciation for how careful, well-managed data can support critical decision-making in research and healthcare settings.

What draws me to this specific role is the chance to put these skills to work in a research environment where the findings genuinely matter. I enjoy the full arc of data work: gathering requirements, structuring and cleaning data, building pipelines, and presenting results in a way that researchers can actually use. Johns Hopkins' commitment to excellence in research aligns closely with how I approach my own work, and I would be proud to support that mission.

Thank you for considering my application. I would love to discuss how my experience in data management, SQL, and research support can contribute to Johns Hopkins University's goals.
