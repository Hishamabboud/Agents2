Dear Hiring Manager,

I am applying for the Data Scientist role at the CDC Foundation's Workforce Acceleration Initiative because the chance to use data to improve public health outcomes is exactly the kind of work I want to do. My brother is a physician and my sister is a psychologist, so I grew up around conversations about patient care, population health, and the real consequences of missing or misleading information. That background shaped how I think about data work: accuracy is not just a technical goal, it is something that affects people's lives. When I read about this role supporting the Illinois Department of Public Health, that felt personal in a way most job postings do not.

At Tekkii, I built and maintained SQL databases across PostgreSQL, SQL Server, and MySQL, designed ETL pipelines, and created complex queries and stored procedures to extract insights for business stakeholders. I built an EDI processing system from scratch for Kansas City Drayage, automating logistics data exchange that had previously been handled manually, which required careful data validation and quality control to ensure reliability. I also transformed the backend and database layer of KidsCloset.biz, cleaning inconsistent records and improving data integrity to the point where the platform was successfully acquired. For KC Sports Directory, I designed and populated a community-facing database supported by the Kansas City Royals Foundation. Across all of these projects, I regularly translated complex technical results into plain language for non-technical stakeholders, which is something this role calls for directly.

At Allied Engineering Group, I worked with SQL Server databases supporting SWIFT transaction processing for more than 400 financial institutions across more than 40 countries, writing optimized queries, maintaining data accuracy, and generating custom reports for clients who depended on that data for critical financial decisions. That experience taught me how to work with high-volume, multi-source datasets and how to keep data reliable at scale. My graduate work at the University of Missouri-Kansas City built on that foundation: for a capstone project, I built a pneumonia detection application using Python and deep learning on medical imaging data, which gave me hands-on experience applying machine learning methods to a real health problem. That project is part of why a role that blends statistical modeling, machine learning, and public health context genuinely excites me.

The WAI program's mission of helping public health agencies build stronger data infrastructure is meaningful work, and I want to be part of it. I am drawn to the collaborative nature of this role, working alongside epidemiologists, program staff, and visualization specialists to turn complex data into decisions that protect people's health. The emphasis on responsible data governance and privacy compliance also resonates with me: I know from experience that the way data is handled is just as important as what it reveals. I would be proud to contribute to the Illinois Department of Public Health's informatics team and to the broader CDC Foundation mission.

Thank you for considering my application. I would love to discuss how my experience in data analysis, machine learning, and building reliable data systems can contribute to the CDC Foundation's work in public health intelligence.

Sincerely,
